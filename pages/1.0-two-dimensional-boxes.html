    Radiance

    A cycles expression can be plotted as a radiance diagram, where all the fixed points occur on the same base index line, but each other cycle has its own points on its own parallel index line - the lines together making a stave.

    Semi-circles, normal to the plane of the stave, connect adjacent points in each cycle, either above the stave, if the sign of the jump (i.e. difference) between the index values for adjacent points is positive.
    • A cycle has a radiance equal to the sum of the absolute values of its jumps.
    • A cycles expression with only fixed points (an identity) has a radiance of 0.
    • A cycles expression has a radiance that is the sum of the radiance of its cycles.
    Or the points underlying the indices in the cycles can be projected on a coordinate grid in two dimensions with lines connecting adjacent points in each cycle to make a perimeter with a euclidean length.
    • A cycle with more than one point has a perimeter equal to the sum of the euclidean lengths of its lines.
    • A cycles expression with only fixed points (an identity) has a perimeter of 0.
    • A cycles expression has a perimeter that is the sum of the perimeters of its cycles.
